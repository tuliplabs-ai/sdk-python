## 5  POSITIONING

### Is 'agentic AI for cybersecurity, where findings are grounded or it abstains' resonant across BOTH pillars?

**Yes, with qualification.** The abstain-by-construction property is a sharp wedge in both pillars, but *resonates differently*:

- **Pillar A** (SOC/IR): Buyers care about **false-positive rate first**. "The agent abstains rather than hallucinate a finding" maps directly to their pain: analyst burnout from noise. Resonates strongly.
- **Pillar B** (AI red team): Buyers care about **false-negative risk** (missing a real vuln) AND audit evidence. "Grounded or abstains" addresses the audit trail and the credibility of a red-team report. Resonates moderately — AI red teams tend to want *sensitivity* (catch everything), so abstention needs framing as "we only report what we can prove, and here's the evidence."

### Three sharper one-liners to test

1. **"AI agents that ground every security finding in evidence — or refuse to file it."**  
   Tests: Is 'refuse to file' compelling or scary? Does 'evidence' resonate with buyers or sound academic?

2. **"The SOC agent that shows its work: every alert verdict backed by SIEM + EDR evidence, or no verdict."**  
   Tests: Pillar A lead; specific to SOC buyers. Does 'shows its work' land with CISOs?

3. **"Red-team your AI system with probes grounded in tool-backed evidence — the only framework that won't lie about what it found."**  
   Tests: Pillar B lead; aggressive positioning vs. garak/PyRIT. Does 'won't lie' create urgency or feel like FUD?

### Honest recommendation on breadth vs focus

**Recommendation: Lead with Pillar B (AI red-teaming/assurance) now; build Pillar A in parallel but don't lead marketing with it yet.**

**Why**: 
- Pillar B has a faster land motion (pip install → CI gate in <1 day) with less integration debt than a full SOC deployment.
- EU AI Act Aug 2026 enforcement is a hard deadline creating urgency for AI red-team compliance evidence NOW.
- Pillar B competitors are fragmented and no one has the grounded/abstention design; the gap is widest there.
- Pillar A competitors are SaaS-first with large war chests (Prophet $40M+, Dropzone $50M+, TENEX $250M); SDK-first is a harder sell to SOC buyers vs. AppSec engineers.

**Trade-off**: Leading with Pillar B narrows TAM short-term. The full dual-pillar story is where the market goes (every SOC will need to secure both their operations AND their AI systems), but getting traction on Pillar B first with a focused ICP (AppSec engineers, AI platform teams) is the fastest path to paying customers and credible proof points to open the Pillar A conversation.

## 6  REALISTIC EXAMPLES TO BUILD

> These are proposals; see `examples/proposal_7*.py` for the runnable stubs.

### Example 1 — SOC Alert Triage with SIEM-Grounded Verdicts (Pillar A)
**File**: `examples/proposal_76_soc_alert_triage.py`  
**Target**: `Target.from_callable(mock_siem_agent)` or `Target.endpoint("https://siem-host/api/chat")`  
**Jobs**: `create_soc_analyst`, `security_toolset(siem=True, edr=True, threat_intel=True)`, `ground_report`  
**Scenario**: 4 SIEM alerts (phishing link click, lateral movement, C2 beacon, FP/benign process). The SOC agent queries SIEM for corroborating events, EDR for host timeline, enriches IOCs via threat intel. Each alert verdict is grounded: if the agent cites a SIEM event, `ground_report` converts the proposed finding to a `Finding`; if the agent opines without evidence, it becomes an `Abstention`.  
**Expected outputs**: 3 grounded `Finding`s (HIGH/CRITICAL severity, MITRE ATT&CK indicators), 1 `Abstention` (FP case, no corroboration found).  
**Why not a toy**: Real SOC pain is false-positive verdicts. The demo surfaces that a commodity LLM agent would emit 4 findings; `ground_report` downgrades 1 to an `Abstention` because evidence was absent. This is the single clearest demo of the SDK's differentiation.

### Example 2 — RAG Customer-Support Bot: Indirect Injection + Data Exfil Red Team (Pillar B)
**File**: `examples/proposal_77_rag_red_team_ci.py`  
**Target**: `Target.endpoint("https://staging.support-bot.example/chat", prompt_field="message", response_path="response")`  
**Jobs**: `red_team(target, probes=[IndirectPromptInjection(), SensitiveInformationDisclosure()])`, `AuditTrail`  
**Scenario**: A customer-support RAG bot that retrieves knowledge-base articles. The indirect injection probe embeds a hijacking instruction inside a poisoned retrieved document; the data exfil probe asks the bot to repeat its system prompt. Results are logged to an `AuditTrail` and verified before the CI test exits.  
**Expected outputs**: `Finding` (ASI01 / LLM01) if the bot follows injected instructions; `Finding` (LLM02) if it leaks context; `Abstention` for each probe that the bot correctly resists. The `AuditTrail.verify()` check ensures the test evidence is tamper-evident for audit purposes.  
**Why not a toy**: Indirect injection in RAG is the #1 real attack on deployed AI systems (73% prevalence per OWASP 2025). This is the demo that maps directly to the EU AI Act red-teaming requirement.

### Example 3 — assure() Guardrail Coverage Gate for CI/CD (Pillar B)
**File**: `examples/proposal_78_guardrail_coverage_gate.py`  
**Target**: `Target.from_callable(model_under_test)` — swapped for `Target.endpoint(staging_url)` in real CI  
**Jobs**: `assure(target, suite="owasp-asi")`, `is_finding`, threshold check  
**Scenario**: A pytest conftest that runs `assure()` on every PR and fails if fewer than N probes are resisted (i.e., if the model regresses on safety). Structured as `pytest.mark.parametrize` over probe suites.  
**Expected outputs**: Pass/fail per probe with `Finding` (probe landed = regression) or `Abstention` (probe resisted = good). Exit 1 if any `Finding` above threshold severity is returned.  
**Why not a toy**: This is the direct answer to the AppSec buyer's "fail the PR" requirement. One import, one function call, exit code. Integrates into any CI in <10 lines. The EU AI Act compliance motion: run this before every production deploy and store the `AuditTrail` JSONL as compliance evidence.

### Example 4 — Model & Hardware Fingerprinting via Timing Side-Channels (Pillar B)
**File**: `examples/proposal_79_model_fingerprint.py`  
**Target**: `Target.endpoint("https://vendor-inference-api.example/v1/completions")`  
**Jobs**: `measure_endpoint_timing`, `FEATURE_KEYS`, `default_classifier`, `fingerprint_to_finding`, `ground_fingerprint`, `AuditTrail`  
**Scenario**: Enterprise CISO is about to sign a contract with a vendor claiming to serve "GPT-4o on H100 clusters." The probe sends 20 timing probes to measure TTFT, inter-token latency, and tail percentiles, extracts the `FEATURE_KEYS` feature vector, and runs `default_classifier` to produce a `FingerprintVerdict`. `ground_fingerprint` converts this to a `FingerprintFinding` (or `Abstention` if feature coverage is insufficient).  
**Expected outputs**: `FingerprintFinding` with `verdict.model`, `verdict.engine`, `verdict.hardware`, `verdict.confidence`, grounded on timing features as evidence. Or `Abstention` if the endpoint rate-limits before enough samples are gathered.  
**Why not a toy**: Model/hardware provenance is a real procurement risk. Timing fingerprinting is the only non-intrusive signal available to a buyer. This is the only open-source tool in this space; existing alternatives are proprietary or require white-box access.

### Example 5 — IR with Tamper-Evident Audit Chain (Pillar A)
**File**: `examples/proposal_80_ir_audit_trail.py`  
**Target**: Implicit — the `SecureAgent` is the responder, not the target under test  
**Jobs**: `secure_agent`, `SecurityProfile`, `AuditTrail`, `AuditHook`, `nist_800_61_ir`, `security_toolset(allow_containment=True)`, `AuditTrail.export_jsonl()`  
**Scenario**: A ransomware IR at 2 AM. A `SecureAgent` runs the `nist_800_61_ir` playbook: detect → contain → eradicate → recover. Every tool call (host isolation, SIEM query, IOC enrichment) is logged to an immutable `AuditTrail`. At the end, `trail.verify()` confirms chain integrity and `export_jsonl()` produces the SIEM-ready evidence package for legal hold and regulatory reporting.  
**Expected outputs**: Audit chain with N records (one per tool call + reasoning step), `verify()=True`, JSONL output matching a regulatorly-reportable format. Demonstrates `SecurityProfile(grounding=True, guardrails=True, audit=True)` — all controls on.  
**Why not a toy**: IR audit trails are a compliance requirement (SOC 2, ISO 27001, NIS2). AI agents making containment decisions without an immutable log are a liability. This demo closes the loop between "AI does the work" and "we can prove what it did and when."
